  • THE PAPER

Obstacle-Avoiding "SmartCar"

Assembled a smart car powered by an Arduino Uno microcontroller that could detect and avoid obstacles as needed. The build utilized distance sensors and basic collision-avoidance algorithms, enabling the car to change directions when faced with a blocked path. The car was programmed to move forward when there nothing was directly in its way, and to turn when something was. This pathing allowed the car to avoid being obstructed by objects in its environment.

  • THE VIDEO

Dynamic Analysis Walkthrough

This project featured a dynamic analysis of a two-link arm system, modeled using MATLAB. Lagrangian mechanics were employed to derive the equations of motion, which were then solved numerically to simulate the system's behavior over time. The analysis provided insights into the arm's kinematics and dynamics. The results were visualized through plots and animations, and I detailed the steps along the way, creating a comprehensive written walkthrough.

  • THE MATH

Optimized Plywood Box Layout

This project focused on maximizing material efficiency by optimizing the layout of boxes cut from a standard 96" x 48" plywood sheet. Using MATLAB, I implemented a Monte Carlo simulation to determine the optimal arrangement and maximize the number of boxes produced while minimizing waste. The results were visualized through graphical outputs, providing clear insights into the effectiveness of the optimization strategy.
